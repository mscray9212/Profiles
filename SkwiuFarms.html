About SkwiuFarms

Skwiu Farms is a future non profit 501(c)3 organization focused on enriching local communities near to the Piedmont and Mountainside of North Carolina through the creation of local cooperative markets based in high quality, free-range poultry, and organically produce grown locally in the nearby area. Skwiu Farms strictly adheres to a no synthetics in the garden policy, a non GMO policy, and we also focus on bringing foods of the native, indigenous Americans to the people in communities adjacent to our projects. Skwiu Farms implements a hands on philosophy for education and outreach, bringing the best of locally grown exotics and natives for a bio-diverse diet from our welcoming community.

Skwiu Farms consists of many people from Permaculture designers, artists, jack of all trade types, apprentices, and scientists. Our unifying force originates in our shared common interest, which is the common interest to all people; That is, to have flourishing, and abundant food growing nearby to ensure our healthfulness and prosperity. Our projects guarantee a mutually beneficial system for the soil microbes and people, and provide carbon sequestration from the atmosphere. We will utilize some known exotic foods, but focus on maintaining a 60% minimum on native plant species. Sections of Skwiu farms are allotted for the conservation of native plants, and restoring the populations of native, and exotic, endangered plants. We expect and openly welcome scientific researchers who want to study these plants, the soil, and videographers or artists who want to document our experiences. We do not discriminate based on anything, however we expect that regardless of your age, you will be able to find productive ways to work, teach, and share.

Our website is a platform through which people can understand more about what we do, or who wish to contact our organization to arrange for accommodations for those staying or visiting. We hope that you will visit our links for our data, and suggest exotic plants for conservation, as well as native plants (that perhaps we've missed or not gotten to yet), so that we have an easier time keeping open documentation of our progress. We will be using the Google Drive, for most of our documents, and therefore need only the capability to cross platform the links from our Google Drive onto the page, either displayed, or linked to. We will be keeping live documentation on the Internet of our inventory, growing and in seed, as well as notes about the growing progress, any pitfalls, and easy growing species that should be noted, with progress on the account of restoration of endangered species' populations.
